Step 3: Misconception & Error Tracking Patterns

💡 Help Tool: Discovering Root Gaps

Look beyond generic letter grades. Group your "not yet proficient" students systematically by the hidden errors they made (e.g., tracking simple computational slips vs. missing whole foundational concept pathways).

Step 4: Targeted Intervention Selection & Execution Plan

💡 Help Tool: Selecting Intervention Modalities

Pick targeted adjustments tailored directly to student needs. Ensure team members use identical strategies so you can easily review outcomes together during your next work cycle phase.

Student Deployment Map

Step 5: Post-Intervention Tracking & Reassessment

💡 Help Tool: Structuring Reassessments

Determine exactly how you will measure recovery. This does not have to be a long written exam—consider focused parallel prompts, verbal check-ins, performance checkmarks, or short adaptive exit tickets.

Step 6: Insights, Strategies & Long-Term Curricular Adjustments

💡 Help Tool: Team Continuous Growth

Reflect openly on team patterns. If certain classrooms excelled at specific targets, share those high-impact instructional practices to refine your upcoming unit plan layouts.
