PLC Collaborative Work Cycle Tool

Based on PLCs at Work® by Solution Tree

Step 1 & 2: Set the Stage & Review Assessment Focus

💡 Help Tool: Facilitator Guidance & Scaffolding

Timeframe: ~4 minutes. Establish your meeting purpose clearly. Review your collective data norms before looking at scores. Identify your primary essential standard, target items, and common proficiency criteria (e.g., 2 out of 3 correct or a 3 on the rubric) before grouping students.

Step 3: Patterns, Errors & Misconceptions

💡 Help Tool: Analyzing Student Performance Data

Timeframe: ~10 minutes. Evaluate learning targets individually. Focus on identifying specific categories of errors rather than just tracking numeric percentages. Group students systematically based on why they missed the mark (e.g., basic calculation errors vs. structural/pathway misunderstandings).

Analyzing the Current Reality (Critical Questions 3 & 4)

Self-assess your team's structural readiness to execute intervention and extensions effectively.

Concept Mastery Area Current Status Assessment Concrete Evidence Description
Process to identify students needing intervention or extension targets
Team execution of specific instructional strategies for tier support
Actionable pathway for managing prerequisite gaps
Process for implementing core reteaching workflows
Clear system for building extended learning opportunities

Step 4: Team-Based Intervention & Extension Strategy

💡 Help Tool: Selecting Intervention Modalities

Timeframe: ~15 minutes. Pick tactical, concrete responses. Group students directly according to their root cause of struggle, and assign corresponding extensions to advanced learners who already hit proficiency targets.

Student Deployment Map

Map students directly to teachers to clarify responsibility for execution.

Step 5: Post-Intervention Monitoring Tools

💡 Help Tool: Reassessment Design Guidance

Timeframe: ~10 minutes. Determine exactly how you will measure recovery. This does not have to be a long written exam—consider focused parallel prompts, verbal check-ins, performance checkmarks, or short adaptive exit tickets.

Step 6: Future Instructional Adjustments & Continuous Improvement

💡 Help Tool: Team Work Cycle Optimization

Timeframe: ~5 minutes. Translate observations into long-term professional gains. Analyze structural gaps in pacing, curriculum clarity, or initial instructional actions to build a sustainable, iterative practice baseline.
